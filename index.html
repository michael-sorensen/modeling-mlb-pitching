GLOSSARY

The "Zone"

The official strike zone is the area over home plate from the midpoint between a batter's shoulders and the top of the uniform pants -- when the batter is in his stance and prepared to swing at a pitched ball -- and a point just below the kneecap. In order to get a strike call, part of the ball must cross over part of home plate while in the aforementioned area. Strikes and balls are called by the home-plate umpire after every pitch has passed the batter, unless the batter makes contact with the baseball (in which case the pitch is automatically a strike).

Strikeout Rate (K%)

Strikeout rate represents the frequency with which a pitcher strikes out hitters, as determined by total strikeouts divided by total batters faced.

Walk Rate (BB%)

Walk rate represents the frequency with which a pitcher walks hitters, as determined by total walks divided by total batters faced.

ERA (Earned Run Avg)

Earned run average represents the number of earned runs a pitcher allows per nine innings -- with earned runs being any runs that scored without the aid of an error or a passed ball. ERA is the most commonly accepted statistical tool for evaluating pitchers.

Whiff %

Whiff Percentages are based on swings / (total swings) (% of swings that miss)

Swing %

Swing Percentages are based on swings / (swings + takes) (% of the time an opposing batter swings)

Fastball %

A fastball is the most frequent type of pitch thrown by a pitcher. The fastball is generally the highest velocity pitch in the game-it typically ranges in speed from the mid-80s to over 100 mph. The metric is a culculation of the number of fastballs thrown divided by total pitches.

Breaking %

A breaking ball (aka breaking pitch) is a pitch in which the pitcher snaps or breaks his wrist to give the ball spin and movement. This includes the curveball, slider, and slurve, but not the various kinds of fastball and change-up or trick pitches like the knuckleball. The metric is a culculation of the number of breaking balls thrown divided by total pitches.

Off-Speed %

an off-speed pitch is a type of pitch thrown in baseball which has a speed and velocity less than that of a fastball. The idea behind this type of pitch is to alter and confuse the timing of the batter. The metric is a culculation of the number of off-speed pitches thrown divided by total pitches

Meatball %

A meatball is "an easy pitch to hit, thrown right down the middle of the plate”. The metric is a culculation of the number meatball pitches thrown divided by total pitches.

First Strike %

The percentage of plate appearances (for batters) or batters faced (for pitchers) that the first pitch was a strike.

Edge %

The "edge" is the borders of the strike zone. The metric measures pitches on the edge divided by total pitches

Ground Ball (GB) %

The percentage of a pitcher’s balls in play that are ground balls, calculated as Ground Balls / Balls in Play

Fly Ball Rate (FB) %

Fly-ball rate represents the percentage of balls hit into the field of play that are characterized as fly balls. Each ball that is hit into the field of play is characterized as a line drive, a fly ball, a ground ball or a pop-up. (A fly ball is a fly to the outfield, while a pop-up is hit to the infield.)

Popup %

Pop-up rate represents the percentage of balls hit into the field of play that are characterized as pop-ups. Each ball that is hit into the field of play is characterized as a line drive, a fly ball, a ground ball or a pop-up. (A fly ball is a fly to the outfield, while a pop-up is hit to the infield.)

Line Drive (LD) %

Line-drive rate represents the percentage of balls hit into the field of play that are characterized as line drives. Each ball that is hit into the field of play is characterized as a line drive, a fly ball, a ground ball or a pop-up.

Pull %

Percentage of batted balls hit to the pull side of the field (e.g. left field for a right handed batter).

Straight Away %

Percentage of batted balls hit up the middle of the field.

Oppo %

Percentage of batted balls hit to the opposite side of the field (e.g. right field for a right handed batter).

In Zone %

Pitches in the strike zone divided by total pitches

In Zone Swing %

Swings at pitches inside the zone divided by pitches inside the strike zone.

In Zone Swing & Miss %

Swings at pitches inside the zone divided by pitches outside the zone.

In Zone Contact %

Number of pitches on which contact was made on pitches inside the zone divided by swings on pitches inside the zone.

Out of Zone %

Pitches outside the zone divided by total pitches.

Out of Zone Swing %

Swings and misses at pitches outside the zone divided by pitches outside the zone.

Out of Zone Swing & Miss %

Swings at pitches outside the zone divided by pitches outside the zone.

Out of Zone Contact %

Number of pitches on which contact was made on pitches outside the zone divided by swings on pitches outside the zone.
